Many families wait until they are overwhelmed before asking for support. But home and community support does not have to be a last resort. It can be a practical way to build stability, protect routines, and make daily life easier to plan around.

Golden Care provides trained support staff for children, youth, and adults with complex needs at home, in the community, and in care settings that need dependable coverage.

## Signs support may be needed

Your family may benefit from home and community support if:

- routines are becoming harder to maintain
- caregivers are exhausted or need planned relief
- outings, appointments, or errands feel difficult to manage safely
- transitions are causing frequent stress or disruption
- the person needs 1:1 support to participate in activities
- life-skills goals need more structure and consistency
- a group home or care setting needs dependable coverage

These signs do not mean a family has failed. They often mean the support plan needs more structure.

## What support can include

[Home & Community Staffing](/community-home-support) can look different depending on the person, the family, and the setting. Support may include:

- 1:1 support at home
- respite for caregivers
- help with routines and transitions
- life-skills and independence support
- community access and outings
- weekend or extended support where available
- support coverage for group homes or similar care settings

The goal is not to take over family life. The goal is to make support more dependable, practical, and easier to plan.

## Who this support is for

Golden Care supports children, youth, and adults with autism, ADHD, developmental disabilities, mental health challenges, and related complex needs. Families, caregivers, private-pay families, referral partners, group homes, and similar care settings can inquire.

Availability depends on region, schedule, support needs, and fit. Home & Community Staffing is offered across Durham, Peel, York, Toronto, and Scarborough.

## What to prepare before reaching out

Before contacting a provider, write down:

- the person's age and support needs
- the region where support is needed
- preferred days and times
- current routines and challenges
- safety or communication considerations
- the type of support you are requesting
- whether the request is for family support or provider coverage

Clear information helps the first conversation move faster and reduces confusion later.

## When to contact Golden Care

Reach out when you need practical support at home, in the community, or in a supported setting. Golden Care can review the request, ask follow-up questions, and discuss whether support may be a fit.

If the situation is an emergency, urgent medical concern, or crisis, contact emergency services or the appropriate crisis support instead of using a website form.
