Choosing a support provider is an important decision. Families, caregivers, and provider partners need more than a friendly conversation. They need to understand how support is planned, delivered, communicated, and reviewed.

The right questions can help you compare options with more confidence.

## Ask what the provider actually supports

Start by clarifying the service. Ask:

- Do you support children, youth, adults, or all age groups?
- Do you provide support at home, in the community, or in care settings?
- Do you provide 1:1 support?
- Do you offer respite or caregiver relief?
- Do you support complex needs?
- Do you support group homes or similar care settings?

Clear service scope prevents confusion later.

## Ask about staff preparation

Families and care settings should understand how support staff are prepared. Ask about:

- First Aid and CPR
- behaviour support training
- de-escalation or crisis response training
- documentation practices
- communication expectations
- supervision and follow-up

Training does not guarantee fit, but it helps show whether the provider takes support seriously.

## Ask how intake works

A good provider should ask questions before accepting a request. Intake may include:

- support goals
- diagnosis or support needs
- safety considerations
- communication style
- preferred schedule
- service region
- funding or payment planning

If a provider says yes without understanding the need, that can be a warning sign.

## Ask about communication

Support is easier to trust when communication is clear. Ask:

- Who does the family or partner contact?
- How are concerns reported?
- How are schedule changes handled?
- Will support notes or updates be provided?
- What happens if the support plan needs to change?

Communication can make or break the experience.

## Ask about fit, not just availability

Fast staffing is not always the same as appropriate support. Families and provider partners should ask whether the support request is a good fit for the person, setting, schedule, and needs.

Golden Care reviews fit before moving forward with [Home & Community Staffing](/community-home-support), Adult Day Program intake, or other service requests.

## Ask what happens next

Before ending the first conversation, clarify:

- what information is still needed
- when you can expect a response
- whether additional review is required
- what acceptance or availability depends on
- whether submitting a form guarantees service

For Golden Care, submitting an inquiry or application does not guarantee acceptance or immediate staffing. It starts the review process.
