Passport funding can be helpful, but many families are not fully sure what it can actually be used for. The simplest way to think about Passport is this: it helps eligible adults with developmental disabilities take part in community life, build skills, plan around goals, and give caregivers access to temporary respite.

This article is for general education only. Families should always confirm current rules with their Passport agency or the official Ontario guidelines before spending.

## What Passport is designed to support

Ontario describes Passport as a reimbursement program for eligible adults with developmental disabilities. Families submit invoices and receipts for admissible expenses, and approved expenses are reimbursed up to the person's annual funding allocation.

Passport may help pay for:

- community classes, recreation, and participation
- work, volunteer, and daily life skill development
- hiring a support worker
- person-directed planning
- temporary respite for caregivers
- supports that help the person participate more fully in community life

## Common support needs families plan around

Families often use Passport planning conversations to think through practical needs such as:

- daytime structure for an adult family member
- community access or supported outings
- support with life skills and daily routines
- respite or caregiver relief
- 1:1 support worker hours
- planning around goals, independence, and participation

## What to organize before using Passport

Before arranging support, gather the basics:

- annual Passport allocation
- who manages the funding
- support goals and priorities
- preferred schedule and region
- invoices, receipts, and service dates
- questions for your Passport agency about eligible expenses

Good records matter because Passport is reimbursement-based. Families should keep documentation clear and confirm current claim rules.

## Questions to ask before spending

Not every expense is covered. Before committing to a service, ask:

- Is this expense connected to the person's support goals?
- Is it admissible under current Passport guidelines?
- Do we have the right documentation for reimbursement?
- Are there claim deadlines we need to track?
- Should we confirm this with our Passport agency first?
